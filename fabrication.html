Fabrication, Smithing & Hybrid Prototyping

Material-first work: forge, shop, CAD, and rapid iteration from early smithing to engineered geometry, repeatable tooling, and small-batch parts.

Before I had access to CNC tools or 3D printers, I learned the basics the old way: heat, force, and iteration.

I started blacksmithing as a teenager on a simple setup heating stock, moving material with intent, and learning what precision means when steel is soft and time is short. That early experience turned into a broader fabrication skill set: patterned metals, CAD-driven parts, 3D-printed tooling, sand molds, and casting.

The common thread on this page is process: design tool test revise. Its the same loop used in R&D and field prototyping just with a wider range of materials.
