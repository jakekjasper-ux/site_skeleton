CNC, Sensors & Mechatronic Interfaces

Printed brackets and interfaces that make commodity sensors usable on real machines.

Reliable sensor integration is almost always a mechanical problem, not an electrical one. Positioning, adjustability, and repeatability under vibration matter more than the sensor itself.

The CNC limit switch mounts shown here were modeled as parametric CAD parts, designed to allow fine adjustment while maintaining repeatable homing behavior. Assemblies were modeled to verify travel limits, fastener access, and service clearance before printing.

Printing here is used to validate interface geometry quickly. Once behavior is confirmed, the same CAD models can be adapted for other materials or mounting conditions.

Industrial & Process-Equipment Prototyping

Adapters and fit-up tooling to align and support mixed-material assemblies in real equipment.

For process equipment, the difficult part is often the interface: aligning new components to existing stainless hardware while preserving serviceability.

The agitator/pasteurizer adapters shown here were designed entirely in CAD as structural fit-up tooling. Assemblies were modeled to control shaft alignment and mounting geometry during testing. Printing allowed rapid iteration until the system ran correctly.

Plastic was never treated as a permanent material here. The CAD and printed parts existed to prove geometry and alignment before committing to final fabrication.

Reusable tooling designed around the realities of packing, disassembly, preheat, and pour.

This work focused on CAD-designed tooling systems, not one-off accessories.

The modular sand mold box system was designed as a parametric assembly, using grooved planks and printed ABS clips to allow packing, ramming, and non-destructive disassembly. CAD modeling allowed separation of the packing stage from the preheat and pour stages â€” a workflow decision made before any material was printed.

The value was not the print. It was the repeatable, reversible process designed into the tooling.
