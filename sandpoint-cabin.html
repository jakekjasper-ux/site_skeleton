Sandpoint Cabin Bathroom Remodel (1960s Log Cabin)

This project involved a full bathroom rebuild inside a 1960s log cabin, where structural movement, moisture control, and limited geometry required coordinated decisions across framing, electrical, plumbing, and finishes.

The original space included a single vanity, tub/shower, and an in-room water heater, with multiple undocumented modifications accumulated over time. Demolition exposed rodent-damaged wiring, compromised subflooring, outdated plumbing, and insulation failures. The scope was not cosmetic â€” it was to identify and correct underlying system issues, then rebuild within the constraints of the existing structure.

The work required treating the bathroom as an integrated system: structure, moisture, electrical, and layout all influencing one another.

Scope Summary

  • Electrical replacement (rodent-damaged wiring removed; accessible runs replaced)
  • Plumbing replacement and water heater relocation
  • Subfloor repair and leveling
  • Insulation upgrades and bath fan venting corrections
  • Waterproofed shower assembly with slate finish
  • Layout revision to improve function within fixed geometry
  • Custom framing for owner-supplied fixtures constrained by existing ceiling and wall structure
