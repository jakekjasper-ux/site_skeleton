Field Service & Troubleshooting

Where installed systems prove themselves — or show their weak points.

I worked in a well pump shop where the same people were responsible for building systems correctly, installing them under real constraints, and servicing them when they aged — across municipal, commercial, and private work.

Field service is where those decisions come back. Often years later. Often under pressure.

Much of the service work shown here involves systems we originally installed or maintained as operator of record — reservoir controls, alarm panels, pump logic, and wiring schemes designed to be diagnosed rather than guessed at.

Typical Conditions

Systems that “worked for years” until they didn’t. Controls altered over time by multiple hands. Marginal splices, drifting sensors, or undocumented changes. Sites where downtime required immediate response.

The objective was not just to restore operation, but to understand why the failure occurred and whether the correction would last.

Representative Field Work

Diagnostics, open panels, pumps, and live systems under load.

PUD & Municipal Work

Utility-scale lifts, diagnostics, and pump house upgrades.

Confined Space & Verification

Confined-space coordination, sanitation, and post-repair validation on drinking-water systems.

Closing

Good field work is not flashy.
It is quiet, methodical, and boring — which is exactly how water systems should behave.
