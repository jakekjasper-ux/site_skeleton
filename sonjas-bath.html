Targeted Tub Failure Analysis & Rebuild

This project involved a localized tub failure inside a recently remodeled bathroom, where surface finishes appeared intact but the underlying assembly had failed structurally.

The fiberglass tub flexed under normal use due to inadequate support. That movement led to cracking, which created a direct water path into the underlayment and apron framing. Because the surrounding finishes remained visually sound, the failure progressed quietly until investigation exposed the true condition.

The constraint was explicit: correct the failure path without dismantling the rest of the room.

System context

The bathroom had already been remodeled and was otherwise performing as intended. The failure was isolated to the tub assembly itself ? specifically the load path and waterproofing beneath the tub. This required treating the tub not as a fixture, but as a structural and moisture system embedded within an otherwise intact assembly.

Observed failure

Selective demolition revealed a simple, traceable sequence:

  • Inadequate bedding and voids beneath the fiberglass tub
  • Tub flex under normal loading
  • Crack formation at the tub base
  • Water migration into underlayment and apron framing
  • Localized degradation of substrate and support

The failure was not cosmetic and not room-wide. It was a single broken load path with predictable consequences.

Approach

The repair was handled as a targeted investigation followed by controlled reconstruction. Work proceeded in the following order:

  • Selective demolition limited to the tub and apron to preserve surrounding finishes
  • Exposure and drying of affected substrate and framing
  • Rebuild of structural support and decking beneath the tub
  • Replacement with a rigid porcelain-coated steel tub to tolerate movement
  • Waterproofing and transition rebuild before tying back into existing tile

At each stage, the guiding constraint was to restore structural and moisture integrity without expanding scope unnecessarily.

Outcome

  • Restored a rigid, continuous load path beneath the tub
  • Eliminated flex that led to cracking and water intrusion
  • Rebuilt waterproof transitions without disturbing intact finishes
  • Final assembly reads as part of the original remodel, not a patch
