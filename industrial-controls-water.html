Industrial Controls & Drinking Water Systems

I worked in a well pump shop where the same people were responsible for building systems correctly, installing them under real constraints, and servicing them when they aged — across municipal, commercial, and private work.

At Gady Pump & Electric, the shop operated under DOH-regulated water system requirements and maintained a UL-508 certified panel shop. Phil Gady was a Class IV Water System Operator, and the shop acted as operator and manager of record for approximately ten Class A and B systems, including small communities, churches, and schools. That responsibility meant immediate action when systems alarmed, failed, or drifted out of compliance.

During my time there, Phil and I were the only licensed workers. I worked as a UL panel shop technician while earning my 03A electrical license, building and servicing controls that had to pass inspection, survive long duty cycles, and remain serviceable years later — because we were the ones called when they didn’t.

The systems shown here include municipal pump stations, reservoirs, school systems, PUD infrastructure, and private installations built to the same operational standards.
